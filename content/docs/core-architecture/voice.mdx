# Voice

Typing is great for precision. But sometimes you just want to talk. OpenClaw's voice system lets you have natural spoken conversations with your agent — hands-free, on any device. Whether you are cooking dinner, driving, or just too comfortable on the couch to reach for your keyboard, voice makes your assistant truly accessible.

## Wake Word Configuration

The wake word is how you get your agent's attention without touching a device. By default, it is **"Hey Claw"**, but you can customize it.

```yaml
# voice/wake-word.yaml
wake_word:
  phrase: "Hey Claw"
  sensitivity: 0.7          # 0.0 (never triggers) to 1.0 (triggers on anything)
  processing: "on_device"   # Wake word detection never leaves your device

  alternatives:
    - "OK Claw"
    - "Claw"                 # Single word, works but more false positives

  custom:
    enabled: true
    phrase: "Hey Atlas"      # Use your agent's name as wake word
    training_samples: 10     # Say it 10 times to train the model

  behavior:
    confirmation_sound: true          # Chime when activated
    confirmation_sound_file: "soft_chime.wav"
    listen_timeout: 10                # Seconds to wait for speech
    cancel_phrase: "never mind"
    ambient_noise_adaptation: true    # Adjusts to background noise
```

A few notes on sensitivity:

- **0.5 - 0.6** — rarely triggers accidentally but you might need to speak clearly and directly at the device.
- **0.7** — the sweet spot for most people. Reliable without too many false positives.
- **0.8 - 0.9** — very sensitive. Good in quiet environments, but might trigger from TV or conversations nearby.

### Custom Wake Word Training

If you want a custom wake word (like your agent's name), OpenClaw trains a small on-device model:

```bash
# Start wake word training
openclaw voice train-wake-word "Hey Atlas"

# The CLI will ask you to say the phrase 10 times in different
# conditions (quiet, noisy, close, far away).

# Test the new wake word
openclaw voice test-wake-word

# Reset to default
openclaw voice reset-wake-word
```

The training takes about two minutes. Say your wake word at different distances and volumes for the best results. The model runs entirely on your device — your voice samples never leave it.

## Talk Mode

Talk Mode is a continuous conversation mode where you do not need the wake word for every exchange. Activate it once, and you can have a back-and-forth conversation naturally, like talking to another person.

```yaml
# voice/talk-mode.yaml
talk_mode:
  activation:
    trigger: "Hey Claw, let's talk"
    hotkey: "hold_volume_button"     # Platform-dependent
    auto_activate: false

  behavior:
    continuous_listening: true
    turn_detection: "voice_activity"  # Detects when you stop talking
    pause_threshold: 1.5              # Seconds of silence = your turn is done
    interrupt_allowed: true           # You can interrupt mid-response

  response:
    voice: "ember"                    # Options: ember, sage, nova, flint
    speed: 1.0                        # 0.5 (slow) to 2.0 (fast)
    pitch: "natural"
    language: "auto_detect"

  deactivation:
    phrase: "That's all, thanks"
    auto_timeout: 300                 # End Talk Mode after 5 min silence
    explicit_only: false              # Also ends on timeout
```

voices:

    description: "Bright, energetic, upbeat"

You can switch voices on the fly:

```
You: Switch to the sage voice.
OpenClaw (now in sage voice): Done. I'll use this voice going forward.
```

## Speech-to-Text Configuration

When you speak, OpenClaw converts your voice to text before processing. You have control over how this works:

```yaml
speech_to_text:
  engine: "whisper-large-v3"
  processing: "hybrid"       # on_device for short, cloud for long
  threshold:
    on_device_max: 30        # Seconds — process on-device up to 30s
    cloud_above: 30          # Longer audio goes to cloud

  language:
    primary: "en"
    secondary: "pl"
    auto_detect: true
    code_switching: true     # Handle mid-sentence language switches

  enhancements:
    punctuation: true        # Add punctuation automatically
    profanity_filter: false  # Don't censor
    domain_vocabulary:
      - "OpenClaw"
      - "ClawHub"
      - "SOUL.md"
      - "Pi runtime"

  privacy:
    audio_retention: "none"  # Don't store raw audio
    transcription_retention: "session"  # Kept for session, then deleted
```

The `domain_vocabulary` setting is important. It teaches the speech-to-text engine to correctly transcribe words it might not know — like "OpenClaw" instead of "open claw" or "SOUL.md" instead of "soul MD."

## Practical Voice Use Cases

### Morning Briefing

```
You: Hey Claw, good morning. What's my day look like?

OpenClaw: Good morning. You've got three meetings today.
First up is a standup at 10 with the product team.
Then lunch with Sarah at 12:30 — she suggested that new place
on Nowy Swiat. Your 1-on-1 with Dr. Chen is at 3.
Between meetings, you have about two hours of focus time.
Oh, and it's 12 degrees and partly cloudy — you might want a jacket.
```

This works beautifully as a voice interaction while you are making coffee. No screen required.

### Hands-Free Cooking

```
You: Hey Claw, let's talk.

OpenClaw: Talk Mode active. What's up?

You: I'm making that mushroom risotto. Walk me through it step by step.

OpenClaw: Sure. First, get your mushrooms sliced and your stock
warming on a back burner. Ready for step one?

You: Ready.

OpenClaw: Heat two tablespoons of olive oil in a wide pan over medium
heat. Add a diced onion and cook until it's soft, about five minutes.

You: Done, what's next?

OpenClaw: Add the arborio rice — about one and a half cups. Stir it
around for a minute until the grains are coated and slightly translucent
at the edges.

You: Wait, should I add garlic?

OpenClaw: Good call. Add two minced cloves right after the onion
softens, before the rice. Cook the garlic for just 30 seconds so it
doesn't burn.
```

Notice how Talk Mode lets you have a natural back-and-forth without saying the wake word every time. And you can interrupt or ask questions mid-recipe.

### Driving Mode

```yaml
# voice/driving-mode.yaml
driving_mode:
  activation: "Hey Claw, I'm driving"
  behavior:
    responses: "voice_only"      # Never show visual content
    length: "extra_concise"      # Keep answers very short
    confirmations: "verbal"      # "Should I send that?" instead of showing a draft
    no_questions: true            # Don't ask clarifying questions, make best guess
    emergency_override: true     # Always alert for urgent messages

  allowed_skills:
    - navigation
    - music
    - calls
    - messages_read_only
  blocked_skills:
    - email_compose
    - code_exec
    - file_manager
```

Driving mode is a safety feature. It restricts what the agent can do, forces voice-only output, and keeps responses ultra-short. Your agent will not ask you to look at a screen.

### Meeting Quick Notes

```
You: Hey Claw, quick note. Sarah wants to move the deadline
to May 15th. Mike pushed back but agreed if we cut scope on
the analytics dashboard. Decision: we'll ship without the
real-time charts and add them in v2.

OpenClaw: Got it. Saved:
  - Sarah proposed deadline change to May 15
  - Mike agreed with condition: cut analytics dashboard scope
  - Decision: ship without real-time charts, add in v2
  Want me to send this summary to the team?
```

Voice notes captured, structured, and ready to share — all while you are walking back from the conference room.

### Bedtime Wind-Down

```yaml
# voice/night-mode.yaml
night_mode:
  activation:
    time: "22:00"
    manual: "Hey Claw, nighttime mode"

  behavior:
    voice: "flint"             # Low, quiet voice
    volume: 0.4                # Reduced volume
    responses: "minimal"       # Very short answers
    no_proactive: true         # Don't volunteer information

  sleep_sounds:
    enabled: true
    trigger: "play sleep sounds"
    options: ["rain", "ocean", "forest", "white_noise"]
    timer: 30                  # Auto-stop after 30 minutes
```

## Troubleshooting Voice

Common issues and fixes:

```bash
# Test your microphone
openclaw voice mic-test

# Check wake word detection
openclaw voice debug-wake-word
# Shows real-time confidence scores as you speak

# Recalibrate for a new environment
openclaw voice calibrate
# Adjusts noise floor and sensitivity for your current space

# Check voice latency
openclaw voice latency-test
# Shows processing time breakdown: STT → processing → TTS
```

If your wake word triggers too often, lower the sensitivity. If it misses your voice, raise it. If speech recognition is getting words wrong, add them to the `domain_vocabulary` list. Most voice issues come down to these three adjustments.

Voice is one of those features that feels gimmicky until you use it in the right context. Cooking, driving, morning routines, quick notes on the go — once you find your use cases, you will wonder how you ever typed everything.
