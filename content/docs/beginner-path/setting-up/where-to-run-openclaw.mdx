Before you install anything, there's one question worth thinking about: where do you actually want OpenClaw to live? Your choice here affects how you use it every day, so it's worth spending five minutes on it now rather than switching things around later.

There are three realistic options. Most students will land on one of the first two, and we'll help you figure out which fits your situation.

## Option 1: Your Own Computer

This is the simplest place to start. You install OpenClaw on your Mac, Windows PC, or Linux machine, run a single command, and you're up and running within minutes. No accounts, no monthly fees, no setup overhead.

The trade-off is straightforward: OpenClaw only works when your computer is on and the terminal window is open. If you close your laptop at night, your assistant goes offline too. For a lot of people — especially those who are just exploring, or who only need their assistant during working hours — that's completely fine. You can always move to a server later if your needs change.

This option is also the best way to experiment without spending any money upfront. If you're not sure yet whether OpenClaw will become a core part of your workflow, start here.

**Who this suits:** People who want the quickest possible setup, students still exploring, or anyone who primarily uses their assistant during the day and doesn't mind it going offline overnight.

## Option 2: A VPS (Recommended for Most People)

A VPS — Virtual Private Server — is essentially a computer sitting in a data centre somewhere that you rent by the month. It runs 24/7, it's always connected to the internet, and it doesn't care whether your laptop is open or closed.

When OpenClaw runs on a VPS, your personal AI assistant is always available. You can message it from your phone, from a friend's computer, from anywhere. It processes tasks in the background while you sleep. That's a genuinely different experience from the local setup.

The cost is lower than most people expect. A VPS suitable for running OpenClaw typically starts at a few dollars a month — less than a cup of coffee.

**Who this suits:** Anyone who wants their assistant always on, people who want to access it from their phone, or students who are serious about building a real AI assistant into their life.

## Option 3: Dedicated Hardware (Raspberry Pi, etc.)

You can also run OpenClaw on a small dedicated device like a Raspberry Pi — a tiny, inexpensive computer that sits on your desk and runs quietly in the background. This gives you the always-on benefit of a VPS without any ongoing monthly cost.

The catch is that setting this up requires more technical confidence. You're managing your own hardware, your own network, and your own security. It's a great option if you're technically comfortable and want full control over everything, but it's not the right starting point for most people coming to this course.

We're not going to cover this option in the beginner path. If it interests you, come back to it once you're comfortable with the fundamentals.

**Who this suits:** Technically confident students who already have some Linux experience and want full local control.

## How to Choose

If you're unsure, here's a simple way to think about it:

Do you want to just try OpenClaw and see if it's useful before committing to anything? Start with your own computer. It takes ten minutes and costs nothing.

Do you already know you want an always-available assistant? Go straight to the Hostinger VPS setup. The course walks you through it step by step, and the difference in experience is significant.

Either way, you can always change your mind later. The setup process is similar enough that switching paths won't feel like starting over.
