# Finding Your First Clients

Let's be honest: the hardest part of starting an AI agent agency isn't the technology. It's finding people willing to pay you money. The good news? Businesses are actively looking for solutions to problems that AI agents solve beautifully. The bad news? They don't know they're looking for *you* yet.

This guide gives you the exact playbooks, templates, and scripts to change that.

## Before You Reach Out: Do Your Homework

Cold outreach only works when it's not really cold. The difference between a message that gets ignored and one that gets a reply is specificity. Before you contact anyone, spend 10 minutes researching their business.

- Visit their website. Note what they sell, how they handle inquiries, and whether they have a chat widget or FAQ page.
- Check their Google reviews. Look for complaints about slow response times, unanswered questions, or poor communication.
- Look at their social media. Are they active? Overwhelmed? Ignoring comments?
- Check their job postings. Are they hiring for customer support, admin assistants, or virtual assistants? That's a signal they need help with exactly the kind of work AI agents handle.

This research takes minutes but makes your outreach dramatically more effective.

## Cold Email Templates

Email is still one of the highest-converting outreach channels for B2B services. Here are three templates you can adapt to your target market.

### Template 1: The Observation Opener

> **Subject:** Quick question about [Business Name]'s inquiry process
>
> Hi [First Name],
>
> I was checking out [Business Name] and noticed you don't seem to have a way for website visitors to get instant answers after hours. Given that [industry-specific stat -- e.g., "68% of real estate leads go to the first agent who responds"], that might be leaving money on the table.
>
> I help [industry] businesses set up AI-powered assistants that handle inquiries, qualify leads, and book appointments around the clock. One of my clients saw their qualified lead volume jump 40% in the first month.
>
> Would a 15-minute call this week make sense? I can show you exactly what this would look like for [Business Name].
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]

### Template 2: The Pain Point Approach

> **Subject:** Saw your team might be stretched thin
>
> Hi [First Name],
>
> I noticed [Business Name] is hiring for a [customer support rep / admin assistant / VA]. Before you commit to a $3,000+/month salary, I wanted to share something that might help.
>
> I build AI assistants for [industry] businesses that handle the repetitive stuff -- answering common questions, scheduling appointments, qualifying leads -- so your team can focus on the work that actually requires a human.
>
> My clients typically see 50-70% of their routine inquiries handled automatically within the first week.
>
> Worth a quick conversation?
>
> [Your Name]

### Template 3: The Results Lead

> **Subject:** How [Similar Business] cut their response time to under 30 seconds
>
> Hi [First Name],
>
> I recently helped a [industry] business similar to yours set up an AI assistant that handles their most common customer questions instantly. Their average response time went from 4 hours to under 30 seconds, and their customer satisfaction scores jumped 25%.
>
> I think something similar could work really well for [Business Name], especially given [specific observation about their business].
>
> I put together a quick 2-minute demo of what this could look like for you. Want me to send it over?
>
> [Your Name]

## LinkedIn Outreach

LinkedIn works best when you've been warming up your presence first. Post about AI agents, share insights about your target industry, and engage with prospects' content before you ever send a connection request.

### Connection Request Message

> Hi [First Name] -- I work with [industry] businesses on automating their customer-facing workflows with AI. Saw your post about [something they posted] and thought we might have some overlap. Would love to connect.

### Follow-Up After Connection (wait 2-3 days)

> Thanks for connecting, [First Name]. I've been working with a few [industry] businesses on something I think you'd find interesting -- AI assistants that handle [specific task relevant to them]. One of my clients went from missing 60% of after-hours inquiries to capturing every single one. Would you be open to a quick chat about whether something like that could work for [Business Name]?

## Twitter / X DM Strategy

Twitter DMs work best when you're already engaging publicly with the prospect's content. Like, reply thoughtfully, and retweet their stuff for a week or two before sliding into the DMs.

### DM Template

> Hey [First Name], been following your content about [topic] -- really good stuff. Random question: have you explored using AI agents for [specific task]? I've been building them for [industry] businesses and the results have been kind of wild. Happy to share what I've seen if you're curious.

Keep it casual. Twitter is not the place for formal pitches.

## The Discovery Call Framework

So someone said yes to a call. Now what? This is where deals are won or lost. The discovery call isn't a sales pitch -- it's a structured conversation designed to understand the prospect's situation, identify pain points, and position your solution as the obvious answer.

### Phase 1: Build Rapport (3-5 minutes)

Don't jump straight into business. Ask about them. Comment on something specific about their company. Be a human being.

- "I saw you just opened a second location -- congrats! How's that been going?"
- "Your Google reviews are fantastic. How do you maintain that level of service?"

### Phase 2: Understand Their World (10-15 minutes)

This is where you ask the questions that reveal the pain. Listen more than you talk.

**Workflow Questions:**
- "Walk me through what happens when a new lead comes in. Who handles it? How fast do they typically get a response?"
- "What does a typical day look like for your front-desk / support team?"
- "What are the most common questions your team answers over and over?"

**Pain Questions:**
- "What happens to inquiries that come in after hours or on weekends?"
- "How many leads do you think you lose because of slow response times?"
- "If you could take one repetitive task off your team's plate, what would it be?"

**Impact Questions:**
- "What would it mean for your business if every lead got a response within 60 seconds, 24/7?"
- "If your team had 20 extra hours a week, what would they spend that time on?"
- "What's a missed lead actually worth to you in revenue?"

### Phase 3: Present the Possibility (5-7 minutes)

Don't pitch features. Paint a picture based on what they just told you.

"So based on what you've described, it sounds like your team is spending about 15 hours a week answering the same set of questions, and you're probably losing 5-10 leads a month because of slow after-hours responses. What if I told you we could set up an AI assistant that handles those routine questions instantly, captures every after-hours lead, and frees up your team to focus on closing deals?"

### Phase 4: Address Logistics (5 minutes)

- "Typically, I can have something like this up and running within 1-2 weeks."
- "I'll send over a proposal with exactly what's included and the investment."
- "Would it be helpful to see a quick demo of what this looks like in action?"

## Objection Handling Scripts

Every prospect has objections. That's normal and healthy -- it means they're actually considering your offer. Here's how to handle the most common ones.

### "It's too expensive."

"I totally understand -- it's an investment. Let me ask you this: you mentioned you're losing about [X] leads per month to slow responses, and each lead is worth roughly [Y] to you. That's [X times Y] in potential revenue you're leaving on the table every month. The agent pays for itself if it captures even a fraction of those leads. And unlike hiring someone, there's no training, no benefits, no sick days."

### "We're not ready for AI yet."

"That's fair, and honestly, a lot of my clients felt the same way before we started. The thing is, this isn't some futuristic technology you need to understand deeply. From your customers' perspective, they're just getting fast, helpful responses. From your team's perspective, they're just getting fewer repetitive questions. You don't need to be 'ready for AI' -- you just need to be ready to stop losing leads."

### "What if the AI says something wrong?"

"Great question, and it's something I take really seriously. The agent is configured to work only with information you've approved. It has guardrails that prevent it from making claims or promises outside its scope. And for anything complex or sensitive, it seamlessly hands off to your human team. Think of it as a really sharp front-desk person who knows exactly when to say, 'Let me connect you with someone who can help with that.'"

### "We tried a chatbot before and it was terrible."

"Yeah, I hear that a lot. Those old rule-based chatbots were painful -- they could only follow rigid scripts and made customers feel like they were talking to a broken vending machine. What I build is fundamentally different. These agents actually understand what people are asking and respond naturally. But I don't expect you to take my word for it -- let me show you a live demo and you can see the difference for yourself."

### "I need to think about it."

"Absolutely, take your time. I'll send over the proposal with everything we discussed so you have it in writing. One thing I'd encourage you to think about: every week without this in place is another week of missed after-hours leads and team hours spent on routine questions. There's no long-term contract, so the risk is pretty low. But I'll follow up next [day] to see where your head is at."

### "Can I just do this myself?"

"You definitely could -- the tools are out there. The question is whether that's the best use of your time. My clients come to me because they'd rather spend their hours on [whatever the prospect's core business is] and let me handle the technical setup, the testing, the optimization, and the ongoing maintenance. It's the same reason you hire an accountant instead of doing your own taxes."

### Following Up Without Being Annoying

The fortune is in the follow-up, but there's a fine line between persistent and pestering. Here's a simple cadence:

- **Day 1:** Send the proposal immediately after the call.
- **Day 3:** "Just checking in -- did you get a chance to review the proposal? Happy to clarify anything."
- **Day 7:** Share a relevant case study or result. "Thought of you when I saw these results from a client in a similar situation."
- **Day 14:** "Wanted to circle back one more time. If the timing isn't right, no worries at all -- just let me know and I'll check back in a few months."
- **Day 30:** Add them to a monthly newsletter or value-based email sequence. Stay on their radar without direct pressure.

The key is that every follow-up should add value, not just ask for a decision. Share an insight, a case study, or a relevant article. Be helpful, and the sales will follow.
