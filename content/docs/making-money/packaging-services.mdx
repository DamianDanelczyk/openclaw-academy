# Packaging Your Services

One of the biggest mistakes new agency owners make is treating every client engagement as a blank canvas. Custom everything, every time. It sounds premium but in practice it's a fast track to burnout, scope creep, and razor-thin margins.

The fix? Packages. Clear, repeatable service tiers that make it easy for clients to say yes and easy for you to deliver consistently. This guide walks you through exactly how to structure yours.

## Why Packages Beat Custom Quotes

When a prospect asks "How much does this cost?" and your answer is "Well, it depends..." you've already lost momentum. Packages solve this in several ways.

**For the client:** They can quickly understand what they're getting and choose the level that fits their budget. No ambiguity, no sticker shock, no three-week proposal process.

**For you:** You can estimate your time accurately, systematize your delivery process, and protect yourself from scope creep. When a client asks for something outside their package, you have a natural upsell conversation instead of an awkward negotiation.

**For your margins:** Packages let you batch similar work together, reuse templates and configurations, and get faster at delivery over time. Your tenth Starter package will take half the time of your first, but you're charging the same rate.

## The Three-Tier Model

After working with dozens of agency builders, we've found that three tiers hit the sweet spot. Fewer than three and you're leaving money on the table. More than three and you're creating decision paralysis. Here's the framework.

### Starter -- $500/month

This tier is designed for small businesses dipping their toes into AI automation. It's simple, effective, and low-risk.

**What's Included:**
- One AI agent focused on a single workflow (e.g., customer FAQ, lead capture, or appointment scheduling)
- Initial setup and configuration (covered in the first month's fee -- no separate setup charge at this tier)
- Integration with one platform (website chat widget, Facebook Messenger, or email)
- Pre-built knowledge base with up to 50 Q&A pairs
- Basic conversation flow with handoff to human when needed
- Monthly performance summary (automated report showing volume, resolution rate, and common topics)
- Email support with 48-hour response time
- Up to 500 conversations per month

**Ideal Client:** Solo practitioners, small local businesses, anyone who wants to automate one specific pain point without a major commitment.

**Your Time Investment:** 4-6 hours for initial setup, 1-2 hours per month for maintenance.

### Professional -- $2,000/month

This is your bread-and-butter tier. It's where the value proposition really clicks for growing businesses, and it's where your margins start to get interesting.

**What's Included:**
- Up to three AI agents covering multiple workflows
- One-time setup fee of $1,500 (covers discovery, custom configuration, and testing)
- Integration with up to three platforms (website, social channels, CRM, email, SMS)
- Custom knowledge base built from client's existing documentation, with up to 200 entries
- Advanced conversation flows with conditional logic and personalized responses
- CRM integration for automatic lead logging and tagging
- Bi-weekly performance review and optimization
- Priority email and chat support with 12-hour response time
- Up to 2,000 conversations per month
- Quarterly strategy call to identify new automation opportunities

**Ideal Client:** Established businesses with a small team, multiple customer touchpoints, and a clear ROI case for automation.

**Your Time Investment:** 12-20 hours for initial setup, 4-6 hours per month for ongoing management and optimization.

### Enterprise -- $5,000+/month

This tier is for larger businesses or those with complex needs. The "plus" in the pricing is intentional -- Enterprise engagements are semi-custom by nature, and pricing scales with scope.

**What's Included:**
- Unlimited AI agents across all business functions
- Custom setup fee scoped per project (typically $3,000-$10,000)
- Integration with all relevant platforms, including custom APIs and internal tools
- Comprehensive knowledge base built from all company documentation, SOPs, and training materials
- Multi-agent workflows where agents hand off to each other based on context
- Advanced analytics dashboard with custom KPIs
- Dedicated account manager (that's you, or a team member as you scale)
- Phone, email, and chat support with 4-hour response time during business hours
- Unlimited conversations
- Monthly strategy sessions with stakeholders
- Custom training for the client's team
- SLA with uptime guarantees

**Ideal Client:** Multi-location businesses, companies with 20+ employees, businesses processing high volumes of customer interactions.

**Your Time Investment:** 30-60 hours for initial setup, 10-15 hours per month for management, optimization, and strategic guidance.

## Presenting Your Tiers

When you present these options, always start with the Professional tier. Behavioral research consistently shows that people tend toward the middle option, and Professional is where your value-to-effort ratio is highest.

Frame it like this during a sales call: "Most of my clients start with the Professional package because it gives them enough coverage to see real results without overcommitting. But let me walk you through all three so you can see what makes sense for your situation."

Never apologize for your pricing. State it clearly, tie it to the outcomes you discussed during discovery, and let the prospect decide.

## Retainer Model vs. Project-Based: When to Use Each

Both models have their place. The key is knowing which one fits the situation.

### The Retainer Model (Recommended Default)

With a retainer, the client pays a fixed monthly fee for ongoing service. This is the model reflected in the tiers above, and it should be your default for most engagements.

**Advantages:**
- Predictable recurring revenue for you
- Predictable costs for the client
- Ongoing relationship means better results over time (you learn their business deeply)
- Natural upsell opportunities as you prove value
- Clients feel "taken care of" -- they have someone on call

**When to use it:** Almost always. Retainers work for any engagement where the AI agent needs ongoing monitoring, optimization, or expansion.

### The Project-Based Model

With project-based pricing, the client pays a fixed fee for a defined deliverable, and the engagement ends when it's delivered.

**Advantages:**
- Clear start and end dates
- Easier for clients who can't commit to ongoing costs
- Can command higher per-hour rates for specialized work
- Good for testing a new market or service offering

**When to use it:** One-off builds where the client has internal technical staff to manage the agent after handoff. Also useful for specific consulting engagements like audits, strategy sessions, or training workshops.

**A Hybrid Approach:** Many successful agencies use project-based pricing for the initial build and then transition clients to a retainer for ongoing management. This lowers the barrier to entry ("Let's start with a $2,000 build and see how it goes") while still creating a path to recurring revenue.

## The Scope Document Template

Every engagement -- every single one -- should start with a scope document. This is not a formality. It's the single most important tool you have for preventing scope creep, managing expectations, and protecting your time.

Here's the template.

---

**Project Scope Agreement**

**Client:** [Business Name]
**Contact:** [Name, Email, Phone]
**Package:** [Starter / Professional / Enterprise]
**Start Date:** [Date]
**Estimated Completion:** [Date for initial setup]

**1. Project Overview**
[2-3 sentences describing what you're building and why. Reference the client's specific pain points from the discovery call.]

**2. Deliverables**
- [ ] AI Agent #1: [Name / Purpose] -- [Brief description of what it does]
- [ ] AI Agent #2: [Name / Purpose] -- [If applicable]
- [ ] Knowledge base with [X] entries covering [topics]
- [ ] Integration with [Platform 1], [Platform 2]
- [ ] Testing and quality assurance
- [ ] Client training session ([duration])
- [ ] Handoff documentation

**3. What's NOT Included**
[This section is critical. Be explicit about what falls outside the scope.]
- Custom API development beyond standard integrations
- Content creation (client provides source material for knowledge base)
- Modifications to client's website beyond embedding the chat widget
- [Anything else relevant]

**4. Client Responsibilities**
- Provide access to [systems, documentation, etc.] by [date]
- Designate a point of contact for questions and approvals
- Review and approve knowledge base content within [X] business days
- Provide feedback on test conversations within [X] business days

**5. Timeline**
| Phase | Description | Duration |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| Discovery | Detailed intake and planning | 2-3 days |
| Build | Agent configuration and knowledge base | 3-5 days |
| Test | Internal testing and refinement | 2-3 days |
| Review | Client testing and feedback | 3-5 days |
| Launch | Go live with monitoring | 1 day |

**6. Investment**
- Setup Fee: $[amount] (due upon signing)
- Monthly Retainer: $[amount]/month (begins at launch)
- Payment Terms: [Net 15 / auto-charge on file / etc.]

**7. Terms**
- Monthly retainer requires 30 days written notice to cancel
- Additional agents or integrations beyond scope will be quoted separately
- Client owns all custom content and configurations created during the engagement

---

Get this signed before you do any work. Email it as a PDF, use an e-signature tool, or even just get a written confirmation via email. The format matters less than the fact that both sides have agreed to what's in and what's out.

One more thing: scope documents are living references, not dusty contracts. When a client asks for something new mid-project, pull up the scope document and say, "That's a great idea. It's outside our current scope, but I can add it to the next phase or quote it as an add-on." No drama, no awkwardness -- just professionalism.
