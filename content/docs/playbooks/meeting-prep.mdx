# Meeting Preparation Assistant

We have all been in that situation. A meeting starts in five minutes, you glance at the calendar invite, and you realize you have no idea what it is about. Maybe you forgot to review the documents that were shared. Maybe you cannot remember what was decided in the last meeting with this person. Maybe you do not even know who half the attendees are.

The Meeting Preparation playbook makes sure that never happens again. OpenClaw automatically prepares a briefing document for each meeting on your calendar, delivered to you with enough lead time to actually read it. No more scrambling, no more bluffing your way through the first ten minutes, and no more "sorry, can you remind me where we left off?" moments.

## What Gets Prepared

For each meeting, OpenClaw assembles a prep package that includes:

- **Meeting context** — What the meeting is about, based on the invite description, agenda, and any linked documents
- **Attendee profiles** — Who is attending, their roles, and your recent interactions with them
- **Previous meeting summary** — What was discussed and decided last time you met with these people
- **Open items** — Any action items from previous meetings that are still pending
- **Relevant updates** — Recent emails, Slack messages, or documents related to the meeting topic
- **Suggested talking points** — Things you might want to bring up based on the context

The depth of preparation scales with the meeting type. A casual one-on-one might get a two-paragraph summary. A client presentation might get a full page with supporting data.

## How It Knows What Matters

OpenClaw uses a combination of signals to figure out how much prep each meeting needs:

- **Meeting title and description** provide the basic topic
- **Attendee list** tells OpenClaw who is involved and what their relationship is to you
- **Calendar patterns** indicate whether this is a recurring meeting or a one-off
- **Your interaction history** with attendees helps surface relevant context
- **Linked documents** in the invite get summarized so you do not have to open them separately

You can also teach OpenClaw which meetings are most important to you. Client calls might always get the full treatment, while internal standups might just need a quick reminder of your updates.

## Setting Up

The key configuration decision is timing. How far in advance do you want your prep delivered? Most users find that 30 to 60 minutes before the meeting works best. Too early and you forget what you read. Too late and you do not have time to absorb it.

You will also want to think about which calendar you want OpenClaw to monitor. If you have separate work and personal calendars, you probably only want prep for work meetings.

## SOUL.md Additions

```markdown
## Behaviors

### Meeting Preparation
- Prepare a briefing for every meeting on my work calendar
- Deliver prep documents 45 minutes before each meeting
- Scale depth based on meeting type:
  - Client/external meetings: Full prep with attendee research, history, and talking points
  - Team meetings: Quick summary of agenda and my pending action items
  - 1:1s: Summary of last meeting, open items, and any relevant updates about the person
  - Recurring standups: Just my status updates and blockers
- Always include a "Last time we discussed" section for recurring meetings
- If the meeting invite has no description or agenda, flag it and suggest I ask the organizer for context
- Summarize any documents linked in the meeting invite (Google Docs, slides, PDFs)
- For meetings with external people I have not met before, include a brief profile based on their LinkedIn or company website
- If I have conflicting meetings, alert me in the prep document
```

## Cron Configuration

```yaml
cron:
  meeting-prep-scan:
    schedule: "0 7 * * 1-5"  # 7:00 AM weekdays — scan today's meetings
    timezone: "America/New_York"
    action: run-playbook
    playbook: meeting-prep-schedule
    config:
      lookahead: 1d  # prepare for today's meetings
      prep_lead_time: 45m  # deliver prep 45 minutes before each meeting
      min_meeting_duration: 15m  # skip very short meetings (under 15 min)
    retry:
      attempts: 2
      delay: 5m

  meeting-prep-deliver:
    schedule: "*/15 8-18 * * 1-5"  # Check every 15 min for prep delivery windows
    timezone: "America/New_York"
    action: run-playbook
    playbook: meeting-prep-deliver
    config:
      deliver_before: 45m
      skip_declined: true  # don't prep for meetings you've declined

  meeting-notes-capture:
    schedule: "*/5 8-18 * * 1-5"  # Every 5 min during business hours
    timezone: "America/New_York"
    action: run-playbook
    playbook: meeting-notes-listener
    config:
      channel: "#meeting-notes"
      auto_extract_actions: true  # pull action items from notes
```

## Optimization Ideas

- **Post-meeting notes**: After each meeting, drop your notes into the #meeting-notes channel. OpenClaw will extract action items and tie them to the next meeting's prep automatically.
- **Prep depth control**: Add meeting-specific rules like "always do full prep for meetings tagged 'client'" or "skip prep for meetings under 15 minutes."
- **Attendee memory**: Over time, OpenClaw builds a profile of everyone you meet with. It remembers conversation topics, preferences, and your relationship dynamics. This makes prep increasingly personalized.
- **Calendar hygiene alerts**: OpenClaw can flag days where you have more than 5 hours of meetings, no breaks between calls, or meetings with no agenda — helping you protect your time proactively.
