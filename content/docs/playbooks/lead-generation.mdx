# Lead Generation Automation

Finding new leads is essential for any business, but the process is often painfully manual. You scour LinkedIn, monitor industry forums, check who visited your website, cross-reference with your CRM, and then try to craft a personalized outreach message before the moment of relevance passes. By the time you get to the outreach part, the lead has already gone cold.

The Lead Generation playbook puts OpenClaw to work as your always-on prospecting assistant. It monitors the sources you care about, identifies potential leads based on your criteria, scores them, and keeps everything organized so you can focus on the human part — actually having the conversation.

## What This Playbook Does

The lead generation pipeline has four core functions:

- **Discovery** — Finds potential leads from sources you configure (social media engagement, website visitors, event attendees, referral mentions)
- **Qualification** — Scores leads based on criteria you define (company size, industry, engagement signals, budget indicators)
- **Enrichment** — Gathers additional context about qualified leads (company info, recent news, mutual connections)
- **Tracking** — Keeps a running log of all leads with their status, last activity, and next steps

Notice that "outreach" is not on this list. OpenClaw can draft outreach messages for you, but it deliberately does not send them automatically. Outreach should feel personal and genuine, so you always have the final say on what gets sent and when.

## Who Should Use This

This playbook is designed for salespeople, founders, freelancers, and anyone who needs a steady flow of new business opportunities. It works especially well if you:

- Have a clear picture of your ideal customer but lack the time to prospect consistently
- Get leads from multiple channels and struggle to keep track of them all
- Want to respond to inbound signals (like someone engaging with your content) quickly

If you are a larger sales team, each member can run their own instance of this playbook with different qualification criteria and territory filters.

## Defining Your Ideal Lead

Before setting up the playbook, get clear on what makes a good lead for you. Think about:

- **Industry**: Which industries are your best customers in?
- **Company size**: Are you targeting startups, mid-market, or enterprise?
- **Role/title**: Who is the decision-maker you want to reach?
- **Signals**: What actions suggest someone might be interested? (visiting your pricing page, engaging with your posts, attending a relevant webinar)
- **Disqualifiers**: What automatically makes someone a bad fit?

The more specific you are here, the better OpenClaw will be at filtering out noise and surfacing real opportunities.

## SOUL.md Additions

```markdown
## Behaviors

### Lead Generation
- Monitor configured lead sources continuously during business hours
- Score each potential lead on a 1-10 scale based on my Ideal Customer Profile
- Only surface leads that score 6 or higher
- For each qualified lead, provide: name, company, role, score, source, and one-sentence context
- Enrich qualified leads with: company size, industry, recent news, and any mutual connections
- Track all leads in a simple pipeline: New, Contacted, Replied, Meeting Booked, Disqualified
- When I say "contacted [name]" or "meeting booked with [name]," update their status
- Draft personalized outreach messages when asked, but never send anything automatically
- Surface warm leads (people who have engaged with my content multiple times) separately — these are higher priority
- Weekly summary every Friday: new leads discovered, pipeline changes, and conversion metrics
```

## Cron Configuration

```yaml
cron:
  lead-discovery:
    schedule: "0 */2 8-18 * * 1-5"  # Every 2 hours, 8 AM to 6 PM, weekdays
    timezone: "America/New_York"
    action: run-playbook
    playbook: lead-discovery-scan
    config:
      min_score: 6  # minimum qualification score to surface
      enrich: true  # auto-enrich qualified leads
      dedup: true  # skip leads already in the pipeline
    retry:
      attempts: 1
      delay: 10m

  lead-weekly-report:
    schedule: "0 16 * * 5"  # Friday at 4:00 PM
    timezone: "America/New_York"
    action: run-playbook
    playbook: lead-weekly-summary
    config:
      include_metrics: true
      include_stale_leads: true  # flag leads with no activity in 7+ days

  lead-followup-reminder:
    schedule: "0 9 * * 1-5"  # Every weekday at 9:00 AM
    timezone: "America/New_York"
    action: run-playbook
    playbook: lead-followup-check
    config:
      remind_after: 3d  # remind about leads not contacted within 3 days
```

## Pro Tips

- **CRM sync**: If you use HubSpot or Salesforce, enable the crm-connector skill to automatically push qualified leads into your CRM. This prevents double entry and keeps your sales manager happy.
- **Outreach templates**: Create 3-4 outreach templates in your SOUL.md for different lead types (cold, warm, referral, event follow-up). OpenClaw will pick the right template and personalize it.
- **Disqualification rules**: Be aggressive about disqualifying leads that do not fit. A clean pipeline with 10 real opportunities is infinitely more useful than a bloated one with 100 maybes.
- **Team handoffs**: If a lead needs to be passed to a colleague, just say "hand off Rachel Torres to @sarah" and OpenClaw will transfer the lead context and notify your teammate.
