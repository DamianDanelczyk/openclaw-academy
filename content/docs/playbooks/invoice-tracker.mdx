# Invoice and Payment Tracking

Cash flow is the lifeblood of any business, but keeping track of who owes you what and when can feel like a part-time job in itself. Invoices get lost in email threads, payment terms get confused, and by the time you realize a client is 30 days overdue, the conversation has already become awkward.

The Invoice and Payment Tracking playbook gives OpenClaw visibility into your invoicing and accounts receivable. It monitors outstanding invoices, sends you reminders before payments are due, flags overdue accounts, and gives you a clear picture of your cash flow at any time. No accounting degree required.

## What This Playbook Tracks

OpenClaw connects to your invoicing tool and monitors the full lifecycle of every invoice:

- **Sent** — Invoice has been delivered to the client
- **Viewed** — Client has opened the invoice (if your invoicing tool supports read receipts)
- **Due Soon** — Payment due date is within 7 days
- **Overdue** — Payment is past the due date
- **Partially Paid** — Client has paid some but not all of the invoice
- **Paid** — Payment received in full

For each invoice, OpenClaw tracks the amount, client name, due date, current status, and days outstanding. It calculates running totals so you always know your total accounts receivable at a glance.

## Why This Matters

Getting paid on time is not just about cash flow. It is about respect and professionalism. When you have a system that proactively tracks payments and reminds you to follow up, you can address late payments early, before they become a pattern. A friendly "just checking in on invoice #247" sent at day 3 past due is very different from a frustrated email sent at day 45.

Many freelancers and small business owners tell us that this playbook alone has reduced their average days-to-payment by 30 to 40 percent, simply because they follow up sooner and more consistently.

## Before You Start

This playbook works best when connected to your invoicing tool. It supports popular options like QuickBooks, FreshBooks, Stripe, Xero, and Wave. If you use a tool that is not directly supported, you can often connect it through a webhook or by forwarding invoice confirmation emails to OpenClaw.

You will also want to think about your follow-up strategy. How many days after the due date do you send a first reminder? A second? At what point do you escalate? Having a clear policy makes the automation straightforward.

## SOUL.md Additions

```markdown
## Behaviors

### Invoice Tracking
- Monitor all invoices from my connected invoicing tool
- Alert me when an invoice has been outstanding for more than 3 days past due
- Send me a weekly summary every Monday with: total receivable, overdue amount, and upcoming payments
- For overdue invoices, suggest a follow-up message that is professional and friendly (not aggressive)
- Escalate to a firmer tone if an invoice is more than 14 days overdue and there has been no response to the first follow-up
- Track payment trends per client — if a client is consistently late, flag them
- Never send payment reminders directly to clients — always draft them for my review
- When a payment is received, confirm the amount and mark it as resolved
- Monthly summary on the 1st: total invoiced, total collected, average days to payment, outstanding balance
- If total overdue amount exceeds $5,000, send an immediate alert regardless of schedule
```

## Recommended Skills

```yaml
skills:
  - name: invoice-reader
    provider: quickbooks  # or freshbooks, xero, stripe-invoicing, wave
    permissions: read-only
    description: Reads invoice data, statuses, and payment history

  - name: payment-monitor
    provider: stripe  # or bank-feed
    permissions: read-only
    description: Monitors incoming payments and matches them to invoices

  - name: email-drafter
    provider: gmail  # or outlook-mail
    permissions: draft-only
    description: Creates follow-up email drafts for overdue invoices

  - name: spreadsheet-writer
    provider: google-sheets
    permissions: read-write
    description: Maintains a running accounts receivable spreadsheet
```

## Cron Configuration

```yaml
cron:
  invoice-daily-check:
    schedule: "0 9 * * 1-5"  # Weekdays at 9:00 AM
    timezone: "America/New_York"
    action: run-playbook
    playbook: invoice-status-scan
    config:
      check_overdue: true
      check_due_soon: true  # flag invoices due within 7 days
      threshold_alert: 5000  # immediate alert if overdue total exceeds this
    retry:
      attempts: 2
      delay: 10m

  invoice-weekly-summary:
    schedule: "0 8 * * 1"  # Monday at 8:00 AM
    timezone: "America/New_York"
    action: run-playbook
    playbook: invoice-weekly-report
    config:
      include_aging: true  # show 30/60/90 day aging breakdown
      include_trends: true

  invoice-monthly-summary:
    schedule: "0 9 1 * *"  # 1st of each month at 9:00 AM
    timezone: "America/New_York"
    action: run-playbook
    playbook: invoice-monthly-report
    config:
      include_client_breakdown: true
      include_collection_rate: true

  invoice-payment-watcher:
    schedule: "0 */4 * * *"  # Every 4 hours
    timezone: "UTC"
    action: run-playbook
    playbook: payment-reconciliation
    config:
      auto_match: true  # automatically match payments to invoices
      notify_on_payment: true
```

## Making the Most of This Playbook

- **Connect your bank**: If your bank supports feed integration or you use Stripe, connect the payment-monitor skill to automatically match incoming payments to invoices. No more manual reconciliation.
- **Client payment profiles**: Over time, OpenClaw builds a payment profile for each client. You will see patterns like "Acme Corp always pays on the 15th regardless of due date" or "BlueHarbor needs a reminder every time." Use these patterns to adjust your expectations and follow-up timing.
- **Deposit and milestone invoicing**: If you bill in installments (common in consulting and agencies), OpenClaw tracks partial payments and knows when the next installment is due.
- **Tax time helper**: At year end, ask OpenClaw for a full summary of all invoices, payments, and outstanding receivables. It exports cleanly to a spreadsheet for your accountant.
- **Cash flow forecasting**: Based on your invoice pipeline and historical payment patterns, OpenClaw can estimate your expected cash inflow for the next 30, 60, and 90 days.
