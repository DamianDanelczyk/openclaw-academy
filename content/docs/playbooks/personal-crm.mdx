# Personal CRM System

Your network is one of your most valuable professional assets, but maintaining it is hard. You meet someone interesting at a conference, exchange cards, maybe connect on LinkedIn, and then life takes over. Three months later, you cannot remember what you talked about or why you wanted to stay in touch. That connection, which could have turned into a partnership, a referral, or a friendship, fades into nothing.

The Personal CRM playbook turns OpenClaw into your relationship manager. It remembers every interaction, tracks when you last reached out, and nudges you when a relationship is going cold. It is not about turning people into leads or contacts into transactions. It is about being the kind of person who actually follows through on "let's stay in touch."

## How It Works

Your personal CRM operates on a few simple principles:

- **Everyone you meet gets logged** — When you tell OpenClaw about a new contact, it creates a profile with whatever context you provide
- **Interactions are tracked** — Emails, meetings, messages, coffees, and any touchpoints you report are logged against each contact
- **Relationships have rhythms** — You set how frequently you want to stay in touch with different people (weekly, monthly, quarterly)
- **Reminders are proactive** — OpenClaw nudges you before a relationship goes cold, not after
- **Context is preserved** — When it is time to reach out, OpenClaw reminds you what you talked about last, what they care about, and any commitments you made

Think of it as having perfect memory for every professional relationship in your life.

## Why a Personal CRM

Enterprise CRMs like Salesforce and HubSpot are built for sales teams managing thousands of accounts. They are overkill for personal relationship management and their interface assumes you are tracking deals, not friendships.

A personal CRM is different. It is lightweight, conversational, and focused on the things that actually matter in a relationship: shared interests, mutual help, genuine connection. You interact with it by telling it things in natural language, not by filling out forms.

## Getting Started

The easiest way to start is by importing your most important contacts. You do not need to dump your entire address book in. Start with 20 to 30 people you genuinely want to stay in touch with. For each person, add:

- Their name and how you know them
- What you talked about most recently
- How often you want to reach out
- Any notable details (interests, family, projects they are working on)

You can add people over time as you meet them. After a conference, a dinner, or an interesting LinkedIn conversation, just tell OpenClaw about the new contact and it handles the rest.

## SOUL.md Additions

```markdown
## Behaviors

### Personal CRM
- Maintain a contact database with profiles for everyone I add
- For each contact, track: name, relationship context, last interaction, interaction frequency target, notes, and tags
- Proactively remind me to reach out to contacts who are approaching their "due" date
- When reminding me to reach out, include: who they are, what we discussed last, any commitments I made, and a suggested conversation starter
- Group contacts by tags: Inner Circle, Professional, Industry, Potential Collaborator, Friend, Mentor
- Allow me to log interactions by saying things like "had coffee with Sarah today, we talked about her new startup"
- Weekly relationship digest: who I should reach out to this week, sorted by priority
- If someone has a birthday, work anniversary, or notable life event that I have recorded, remind me a day before
- Never reach out to anyone on my behalf — just remind me and suggest talking points
- Monthly stats: how many people I stayed in touch with, who I have been neglecting, and any relationships that need attention
- When I meet someone new, suggest an appropriate follow-up cadence based on the relationship type
```

## Recommended Skills

```yaml
skills:
  - name: contact-manager
    provider: openclaw-native
    description: Stores and manages contact profiles and interaction history

  - name: calendar-reader
    provider: google-calendar  # or outlook-calendar
    permissions: read-only
    description: Detects meetings with contacts and logs them as interactions

  - name: email-scanner
    provider: gmail
    permissions: read-only
    description: Detects email exchanges with contacts and logs them

  - name: linkedin-connector
    provider: openclaw-native
    config:
      mode: passive  # only reads, never posts or messages
    description: Monitors LinkedIn for contact updates and life events

  - name: reminder-engine
    provider: openclaw-native
    description: Schedules and delivers relationship reminders
```

## Cron Configuration

```yaml
cron:
  crm-daily-reminders:
    schedule: "0 8 * * 1-5"  # Weekdays at 8:00 AM
    timezone: "America/New_York"
    action: run-playbook
    playbook: crm-daily-check
    config:
      remind_approaching: true  # contacts due within 3 days
      remind_overdue: true  # contacts past their cadence
      include_events: true  # birthdays, anniversaries
    retry:
      attempts: 1
      delay: 10m

  crm-weekly-digest:
    schedule: "0 9 * * 1"  # Monday at 9:00 AM
    timezone: "America/New_York"
    action: run-playbook
    playbook: crm-weekly-digest
    config:
      top_priority: 10  # show top 10 contacts to reach out to
      include_recent: true  # also show recent interactions for context

  crm-monthly-review:
    schedule: "0 10 1 * *"  # 1st of each month at 10:00 AM
    timezone: "America/New_York"
    action: run-playbook
    playbook: crm-monthly-stats
    config:
      include_neglected: true  # contacts with no interaction in 60+ days
      include_network_growth: true

  crm-interaction-sync:
    schedule: "0 20 * * *"  # 8 PM daily
    timezone: "America/New_York"
    action: run-playbook
    playbook: crm-auto-log
    config:
      scan_calendar: true  # auto-log meetings as interactions
      scan_email: true  # auto-log email exchanges
      require_confirmation: false  # log silently, don't ask for each one
```

## Building Your CRM Over Time

- **Quick logging**: After any interaction, just message OpenClaw something like "caught up with Maria at the conference, she's pivoting to healthcare tech, wants an intro to our CTO." OpenClaw logs everything.
- **Smart follow-up cadence**: Not everyone needs the same frequency. Your five closest professional relationships might be weekly. Industry acquaintances might be quarterly. OpenClaw helps you set and adjust these based on actual interaction patterns.
- **Event-based reach outs**: When OpenClaw detects that a contact changed jobs, published an article, or had a company milestone, it suggests reaching out with something relevant rather than a generic "how are you."
- **Relationship scoring**: Over time, OpenClaw can show you which relationships are strongest (frequent, mutual interactions) and which are one-sided (you always initiate). This helps you invest your energy wisely.
- **Group outreach**: Planning a dinner or event? Ask OpenClaw to suggest a guest list based on contacts in a specific tag who live in your city and who you have not seen in a while.
