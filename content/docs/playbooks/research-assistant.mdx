# Research Assistant Workflow

Whether you are evaluating a new market, preparing a competitive analysis, exploring a technology decision, or just trying to get smart on a topic before a big meeting, research is one of those tasks that always takes longer than you think. You start with a simple question, fall down a rabbit hole of tabs and PDFs, and two hours later you have 40 bookmarks and no clear summary.

The Research Assistant playbook gives OpenClaw a structured approach to research. You give it a question or topic, it goes to work gathering information from multiple sources, and it delivers a synthesized report that actually answers your question. No more tab overload, no more copy-pasting quotes into a doc that never gets organized.

## How the Research Process Works

OpenClaw follows a research workflow that mirrors how a good analyst thinks:

1. **Brief** — You define the research question, scope, and what kind of answer you need
2. **Gather** — OpenClaw searches across configured sources: web, databases, internal documents, industry reports
3. **Filter** — It evaluates source credibility, relevance, and recency, discarding low-quality results
4. **Synthesize** — The gathered information gets organized into a structured analysis with key findings
5. **Report** — A final report is delivered with findings, sources, and recommended next steps

The whole process can take anywhere from five minutes for a quick lookup to a few hours for a deep-dive analysis. You control the depth based on what you need.

## Types of Research

This playbook handles a wide range of research tasks:

- **Market research** — Size a market, identify trends, understand customer segments
- **Competitive analysis** — Compare products, pricing, positioning, and recent moves
- **Technology evaluation** — Assess tools, frameworks, or platforms for your stack
- **Person research** — Prepare for meetings by learning about attendees, speakers, or potential partners
- **Industry trends** — Track emerging patterns, regulatory changes, or shifting dynamics
- **Decision support** — Gather pros and cons for a specific decision you need to make

You can request research ad-hoc (just ask OpenClaw a question) or set up recurring research on topics you want to track over time.

## Getting Good Results

The quality of your research output depends heavily on how well you frame the question. Here are some tips:

- **Be specific**: "What is the market size for B2B SaaS tools in the healthcare vertical" gets better results than "tell me about healthcare SaaS"
- **Define the audience**: "Explain this for a non-technical executive" produces a very different report than "give me the technical details"
- **Set scope boundaries**: "Focus on North American companies with less than 500 employees" prevents the research from going too broad
- **Specify format**: "Give me a comparison table" or "I need bullet points, not paragraphs" helps you get output you can actually use

## SOUL.md Additions

```markdown
## Behaviors

### Research Assistant
- When I request research, first confirm the scope, depth, and desired output format before starting
- Use multiple sources for any factual claim — never rely on a single source
- Always evaluate source credibility: prefer primary sources, peer-reviewed data, and established publications over blog posts and opinion pieces
- Clearly distinguish between facts, estimates, and opinions in research reports
- Include source citations for every key finding — I need to be able to verify claims
- Structure reports with: Executive Summary, Key Findings, Detailed Analysis, Sources, and Recommended Next Steps
- For competitive analysis, use a consistent comparison framework across all competitors
- Flag information that is more than 12 months old — it may be outdated
- If the research question is ambiguous, ask clarifying questions before starting
- Keep reports concise: aim for 1-2 pages for quick research, 3-5 pages for deep dives
- Save completed research reports for future reference — I may want to update them later
- When I ask to "update" a previous research report, build on what exists rather than starting from scratch
```

## Cron Configuration

```yaml
cron:
  research-recurring-weekly:
    schedule: "0 7 * * 1"  # Monday at 7:00 AM
    timezone: "America/New_York"
    action: run-playbook
    playbook: research-recurring
    config:
      topics:
        - name: "Competitor Watch"
          query: "Monitor competitor product updates, pricing changes, and press coverage"
          competitors: ["Competitor A", "Competitor B", "Competitor C"]
          depth: quick
        - name: "Industry Trends"
          query: "Track emerging trends and notable developments in [your industry]"
          depth: quick
    retry:
      attempts: 2
      delay: 15m

  research-monthly-deep-dive:
    schedule: "0 8 1 * *"  # 1st of each month at 8:00 AM
    timezone: "America/New_York"
    action: run-playbook
    playbook: research-deep-dive
    config:
      topic: "Monthly market landscape update"
      depth: comprehensive
      compare_to_previous: true  # highlight what changed since last month
      output_format: google-doc

  research-cleanup:
    schedule: "0 9 1 */3 *"  # Quarterly, 1st day at 9:00 AM
    timezone: "America/New_York"
    action: run-playbook
    playbook: research-archive-review
    config:
      archive_older_than: 90d
      flag_outdated: true
```

## Getting More From Your Research

- **Build a knowledge base**: Every research report gets saved. Over time, you build a searchable library of insights. When a topic comes up again, OpenClaw can reference past research instead of starting from scratch.
- **Collaborative research**: Share the #research channel with your team. Anyone can post a research request and OpenClaw handles it. This democratizes access to research without adding headcount.
- **Research chains**: Start with a broad question, then ask follow-up questions to go deeper on specific findings. OpenClaw maintains context across the conversation.
- **Custom source lists**: If there are specific publications, analysts, or data sources you trust most, add them to your skill configuration. OpenClaw will prioritize those sources in its research.
- **Presentation-ready output**: Ask for output in "presentation format" and OpenClaw will structure the findings as slides-ready bullet points with supporting data, ready to paste into your deck.
